About me.

What do currently do?

I am a person who gets stuff done, and my ‘superpower’ is the ability to thrive on change, new challenges and new tasks. I am primarily a Customer Success Guru for the D-A-CH market, butI regularly help out in the Portuguese Queue and the Dutch Queue, and work on projects and ideas with people across other departments.

Why Themes?

Since the first day of training when starting as a Guru, I’ve been wanted to be part of Themes Support (ask my trainer Darren - I’ve been going on and on about it ever since!). What draws me to apply for the position of Themes Support Specialist is my wish to write amazing code, help merchants to improve the appearance of their stores, and share my enthusiasm about programming with Guruland.

Why am I perfect for the position?

Ever since I’ve started programming around 1,5 years ago I’ve been obsessed with trying out different languages and learning additional skills, and I constantly try to improve. I don’t shy away from new challenges and from learning new skills in order to successfully complete new tasks, whether they are of a technical nature or not. I thrive on feedback and use it to grow, and I am not afraid to provide it to others. I like to see feedback as an opportunity, and treat it like a problem solving session in which two or more people work together to find a great solution to something that’s not ideal yet.

Merchant Growth Conversations in General Support vs. Language Care

Merchants across the globe come to our support in order to ask for feedback on their store, or direct marketing advice. As Gurus we aim to incorporate a merchant growth conversation into every interaction. As Language Care Gurus we are experts in our market and understand the cultural background that prompts different questions, however, on a subjective level it seems like we have less options to do so on the same level as general support With this project we are aiming to gain an understanding of the difference in the implementation of Business Coaching/ Feature Fit (Merchants Growth conversations in general), and the difference in how many/ what resources are being utilised in comparison between General Support and Language care..
